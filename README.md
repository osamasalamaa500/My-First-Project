This project analyzes leadership energy sustainability and burnout visibility using survey data from Domain 2: Leadership Energy & Performance Capacity within the Rise 1 Million Global Insights initiative. The objective was to describe how organizations perceive leadership performance sustainability, confidence in maintaining leadership pace, presence of burnout-tracking systems, and the extent to which fatigue is linked to strategic errors.
Using descriptive analytics and visualization techniques, we examined four assigned survey questions (Q7, Q8, Q9, and Q11). Results show that most organizations do not formally measure leadership energy sustainability or burnout indicators, with a large concentration of responses indicating uncertainty or lack of tracking. Confidence in leaders maintaining their current performance pace is generally moderate rather than high, suggesting variability in perceived long-term sustainability.
The Power BI dashboard translates these findings into an executive-level visual summary, highlighting key distributions and response patterns. Rather than diagnosing causes or proposing organizational changes, the analysis identifies measurement gaps and notable response patterns that may guide future phases of the global study.
Overall, the findings suggest that leadership performance is widely monitored, but the human energy supporting that performance is rarely measured, creating limited visibility into burnout risk at an organizational level.
