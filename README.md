🔍 About the Project
This project was completed as part of the Rise 1 Million Global Insights initiative — a real-world industry engagement through Riipen. The goal of the initiative is to build the world's first global index of Human Energy Sustainability, helping organizations understand leadership capacity, burnout risk, and long-term performance stability.
Our team was assigned Domain 2: Leadership Energy & Performance Capacity, focusing on four pilot survey questions:
QuestionTopicQ7Sustainable energy vs. exhaustion fuelling performanceQ8Confidence in leaders maintaining pace for 12 monthsQ9Presence of burnout or human energy tracking indicatorsQ11Percentage of strategic errors linked to leader fatigue
The analysis involved descriptive analytics, index construction, and an interactive Power BI dashboard presented as an executive-level visual summary. The project scope was explicitly descriptive — no causal claims were made, and no prescriptive organizational recommendations were offered.

✨ Project Highlights

87.85% of survey respondents reported they do not measure whether leadership performance is fuelled by sustainable energy or exhaustion — revealing a critical blind spot in organizational health monitoring
Only 1.87% of organizations formally tracked burnout indicators, with the overwhelming majority reporting no tracking or early exploration only
99.07% of respondents reported that strategic errors linked to leader fatigue were "not tracked," highlighting a near-complete absence of fatigue-impact visibility at the organizational level
Constructed an index-based scoring framework that converts subjective survey perceptions into four consistent, comparable wellness metrics suitable for cross-organizational benchmarking
Delivered an executive Power BI dashboard surfacing response distributions, risk zone classifications, and maturity levels across all four leadership energy dimensions
